PRECAUTIONS

Practice Good Hygiene

  • Wash your hands frequently: Wash your hands thoroughly with soap and water, especially after coughing, sneezing, or touching surfaces.
  • Cover coughs and sneezes: Use a tissue or your elbow to cover your mouth and nose when coughing or sneezing.
  • Avoid touching your face: Reduce the risk of spreading germs by avoiding touching your eyes, nose, and mouth.

    • Get Vaccinated

    Pneumonia can be prevented by two types of vaccines: the pneumococcal conjugate vaccine (PCV) and the pneumococcal polysaccharide vaccine (PPSV).

  • Get the flu vaccine: Annual flu vaccination can help prevent pneumonia caused by the flu.
  • Get pneumonia vaccines: Vaccines are available for certain types of pneumonia caused by bacteria and can help prevent serious infections.

    • Maintain a Healthy Lifestyle

  • Quit smoking: Smoking damages the lungs and makes them more vulnerable to infection, increasing the risk of pneumonia.
  • Eat a healthy diet: A balanced diet can help support a strong immune system.
  • Get regular exercise: Physical activity helps improve overall health and boost the immune system.
  • Manage chronic conditions: If you have conditions like asthma, COPD, diabetes, or heart disease, work with your doctor to manage them effectively.

    • DIET PLAN

    • Protein-rich foods: Beans, lentils, nuts, dairy products, fish, poultry, and lean meats are excellent sources of protein, which is essential for tissue repair and immune function.
    • Fruits and vegetables: Green leafy vegetables (kale, spinach, lettuce), citrus fruits (orange, lemon, kiwi), and other colorful vegetables are packed with vitamins and minerals that strengthen the immune system.
    • Whole grains: Oats, brown rice, and whole wheat products provide energy and fiber to support digestion and overall health.
    • Hydration: Water, coconut water, fresh fruit juices, herbal teas, soups, and broths are all good options for staying hydrated, which helps loosen mucus and flush out toxins.
    • Probiotics: Yogurt contains beneficial bacteria that can aid in digestion and immune function.
    • Other helpful foods: Honey, ginger, turmeric, and garlic are known for their anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which can help fight the infection and ease symptoms.

    EXERCISES

    Diaphragmatic Breathing

  • Inhale: Breathe in slowly through the nose, allowing your belly to expand as the diaphragm contracts and pulls air into the lungs.
  • Exhale: Breathe out slowly through the mouth, allowing your belly to retract as the diaphragm relaxes.
  • Focus: Pay attention to the rising and falling of your belly, keeping your shoulders and neck relaxed.

    • Pursed-Lip Breathing

    Pursed-lip breathing is a respiratory technique used to slow down breathing and improve airflow during exhalation, particularly beneficial for individuals with chronic lung conditions like COPD or asthma.

  • Sit comfortably and inhale slowly through your nose.
  • Exhale slowly through pursed lips (as if whistling).
  • Keep your mouth closed and exhale slowly, with a slight pressure on your lips.
  • Repeat 5-10 times.

    • Deep Breathing with Coughing

    Deep breathing and coughing exercises help clear airways, loosen mucus, and improve lung function. These exercises involve taking deep breaths and then forcefully coughing to expel mucus and other secretions.

  • Inhale deeply through your nose, filling your lungs.
  • Hold your breath for a few seconds.
  • Exhale slowly and deliberately through pursed lips.
  • Follow with a forceful cough to clear mucus.
  • Repeat this sequence several times.
