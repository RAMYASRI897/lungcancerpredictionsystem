PRECAUTIONS

Avoid Triggers

  • Stay away from smoke, dust, and allergens.
  • Stay Away From Allergens.
  • Avoid Smoke of Any Type.
  • Prevent Colds.
  • Allergy-Proof Your Home.
  • Get Your Vaccinations.
  • Consider Immunotherapy Allergy Shots
  • Take Asthma Medications as Prescribed

    • Use Inhaler Correctly
  • Prepare the inhaler: Remove the cap and shake the inhaler before each use.
  • Breathe out fully: Exhale completely before starting to inhale the medication.
  • Inhale and press: Begin breathing in slowly through your mouth while simultaneously pressing down on the inhaler to release the medication
  • Hold your breath: Once you have inhaled the medication, hold your breath for usually 5-10 seconds.Exhale gently.
    • Monitor Symptoms

    Key points about monitoring asthma symptoms:

  • Common symptoms Wheezing, coughing (especially at night), chest tightness, shortness of breath, difficulty breathing.
  • Peak flow meter A device used to measure the force of your breath, with lower readings signifying potential asthma worsening.
  • Symptom diary
  • Nighttime symptom
  • Communication with healthcare provider

    • DIET PLAN

    Asthma-Friendly Foods

    • Fruits: Apples, Berries, Bananas
    • Vegetables: Spinach, Carrots, Broccoli
    • Omega-3 Rich Foods: Salmon, Walnuts, Flaxseeds
    • Fruits and vegetables: Leafy greens like spinach and broccoli Bell peppers, blueberries, sweet potatoes, and raspberries Avocados Apples, which are rich in antioxidants
    • Whole grains: Cereals, pasta, rice, and potatoes Beans and pulses Chick peas, kidney beans, cannellini beans, butter beans, lentils, black eyed beans, borlotti beans, and haricot beans
    • Fish : Salmon, tuna, and sardines, which are rich in omega-3 fatty acids
    • Other foods: Ginger, which may help relax the airways Foods rich in vitamin D, such as milk, eggs, and fish Dietary tips: Eat more fruits and vegetables Avoid processed foods Eat lean protein Get enough vitamin D Cook with olive or canola oil instead of butter Flavor with herbs instead of salt

    EXERCISES

    Yoga

    Improve breathing and relaxation with yoga. Bridge pose, Baby cobra pose, Supine spinal twist, Cat-cow pose, Low lunge pose

  • Bridge pose: Opens the chest and airways
  • Baby cobra: Improves spinal flexibility and deep breathing
  • Supine spinal twist: Improves deep breathing and digestion
  • Alternate nostril breathing: Relieves stress and shortens shortness of breath
  • Victorious breathing: Improves lung function and relaxation

    • Swimming
  • Swimming when you have asthma is a good idea. It increases physical fitness that might otherwise be impossible for asthma sufferers. Swimming provides the perfect conditions for good breathing and it increases lung function.
  • Swimming is one of the most recommended exercises for people with asthma. Compared to other activities, it's less likely to cause asthma-related symptoms due to: moist, warm air. low pollen exposure.
  • Moist air environment
  • Controlled breathing
  • Improved lung function
  • Low-impact exercise
  • Upper body muscle engagement
    • Walking
  • Asthma can be triggered by common illnesses and lifestyle factors, many of which can be prevented or managed by getting more active. That's why adding regular walks to your routine can reduce your symptoms by increasing your lung capacity, strengthening your immune system, and helping you maintain a healthy weight.
    • Benefits of walking for asthma
  • Improves lung capacity: Walking increases the amount of oxygen your body can use.
  • Strengthens immune system: Being active can help prevent or manage illnesses that trigger asthma.
  • Maintains a healthy weight: Walking can help you maintain a healthy weight.
  • Reduces stress: Walking can boost your mood and help reduce stress.
