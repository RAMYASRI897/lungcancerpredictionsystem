PRECAUTIONS

Administrative Controls

  • These measures aim to reduce the risk of exposure to individuals who may have active TB.
  • Screening: Identifying and isolating individuals with active TB to prevent transmission.
  • Patient management: Implementing protocols for managing patients with TB in healthcare settings.
  • Employee training: Educating healthcare workers on TB prevention and control practices.

    • Environmental Controls
  • These measures focus on preventing the spread of TB bacteria by minimizing the concentration of droplet nuclei. Examples include
  • Good ventilation: Ensuring adequate air exchange to dilute TB bacteria in the air.
  • Natural light: UV light can help kill TB bacteria.
  • Infection control practices: Implementing procedures to prevent the spread of TB bacteria in healthcare settings.
    • Respiratory Protection

  • This involves using respiratory protective equipment to further reduce the risk of exposure in specific situations. Examples include:
  • Masks: Wearing N95 respirators or other appropriate masks when in close contact with individuals suspected or known to have TB.
  • Negative pressure rooms: Isolating patients with TB in rooms with negative pressure to prevent air from the room from entering adjacent areas.

    • DIET PLAN

    Key Components of a TB Diet

    • High Protein Intake: Protein is essential for tissue repair and maintaining muscle mass, especially during TB recovery. Include lean meats, eggs, poultry, fish, beans, lentils, and nuts.
    • Micronutrients: Vitamins and minerals are vital for immune function and overall health. Prioritize fruits, vegetables, and whole grains.
    • Calorie-Rich Foods: TB patients often require more calories to support healing and energy needs. Include foods like avocados, nuts, seeds, and whole milk.
    • Iron-Rich Foods: TB can increase the risk of anemia, so include iron-rich foods like red meat, poultry, fish, beans, and leafy greens.
    • Superfoods: Include foods like turmeric, garlic, ginger, and green tea, known for their anti-inflammatory and immune-boosting properties.
    • Healthy Fats: Include avocados, nuts, and seeds in your diet for healthy fats and calorie intake.

      • Foods to Limit or Avoid

    • Sugary Drinks and Processed Snacks: These can weaken the immune system.
    • Refined Carbohydrates and Processed Foods: These offer little nutritional value and can negatively impact digestion.
    • Excessive Caffeine and Alcohol: These can interfere with the body's ability to heal.
    • Deep-fried Foods: These can be difficult to digest and may worsen symptoms.

    EXERCISES

    Low-Intensity Cardio

    Activities like walking, cycling, or swimming at a low intensity can improve cardiovascular health and lung capacity. These exercises can be gradually increased in duration and intensity as fitness improves.

  • Walking: A great starting point for building endurance, walking at a comfortable pace for 30-60 minutes can be beneficial.
  • Cycling: Provides a low-impact workout for the heart and legs, and can be done indoors or outdoors.
  • Swimming: A full-body workout that is easy on the joints due to the water's buoyancy.
  • Elliptical Training: Similar to running or cycling, but with less impact on the joints.
  • Low-Impact Aerobics: Classes or routines designed to be gentle on the body, like tai chi or water aerobics.

    • Yoga
  • Kapalbhati (Breath of Fire): This pranayama involves forceful exhalations, which can help clear respiratory passages and expel allergens and infectious materials.
  • Bhastrika (Bellows Breathing): This technique strengthens the lungs and improves respiratory capacity.
  • Bhujangasana (Cobra Pose): This asana helps improve spinal flexibility and lung capacity.
  • Diaphragmatic Breathing (Belly Breathing): This technique focuses on using the diaphragm for breathing, which can help improve lung function and reduce fatigue.
    • Breathing Exercises
  • Exercises like pursed-lip breathing and diaphragmatic breathing can help reduce respiratory load, improve gas exchange, and lower respiratory rate.
  • Pursed-Lip Breathing (PLB): This technique involves inhaling through the nose and exhaling slowly through pursed lips, like you're whistling. It helps to keep airways open during exhalation, prevents airway collapse, and slows down the breathing rate.
  • Diaphragmatic Breathing (DB): This exercise focuses on using the diaphragm, the primary muscle for breathing, instead of relying on accessory muscles in the chest and shoulders. It increases lung capacity, improves oxygen intake
